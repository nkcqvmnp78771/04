A moment worth more than paper profits for Anthony Pratt


It was the moment that Australia’s richest man had been waiting for and working assiduously to ­deliver. Anthony Pratt, Australia’s red-haired cardboard king, brought Scott Morrison and Donald Trump together in the hometown of the first man to walk on the moon.

As they walked the floor of Mr Pratt’s new $US500m ($739m) paper plant in Ohio’s Wapakon­eta, the political power pair extolled­ the virtues of his vision: to revive manufacturing and create new, highly paid blue-collar jobs in the US Midwest.


The attendance of the US President and the Prime Minister at the opening of the plant also symbolised a new high point in Australia-US diplomatic ties and a deepening of the personal relationship between the leaders.

The moment had its genesis when the billionaire businessman made his now famous $100,000 bet in October 2016 that Mr Trump, then a political outsider, would win the US presidential election.

That unexpected victory led to a public pledge in 2017 that Mr Pratt’s cardboard box-making and recycling company would spend $2bn in America over the next decade, driving thousands of jobs in the American Midwest.
